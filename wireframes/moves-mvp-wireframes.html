MVP thesis

Core objects
  • Post: photo/video/text attached to a move.
  • Move: event with host, date, place, visibility, RSVP.
  • Circle: lightweight group for friends or interest crews.

Map discovery

  • Use Google Maps for a fast MVP search surface: events near me, venues, neighborhoods, and directions.
  • Overlay Moves-specific pins for RSVP price, friend interest, and hosted-by-friend signals.
  • Keep the event list below the map so users can scan details without losing location context.

Near-me data needed

Ask for location permission, geocode event venues, store latitude/longitude on each move, then sort by distance and time. V1 can open full directions in Google Maps after RSVP.

Detail page priority

  • Date, location, price, host, and friend attendance must be visible before the fold.
  • RSVP creates the social unlock: guest list, comments, and chat.
  • Paid ticketing can be deferred to Stripe Checkout or a hosted payment link for MVP.

Important states

Public, friends-only, invite-only, sold out, waitlist, canceled, and past event. A social app needs those states as much as a ticketing app does.

Host MVP
